Astrobiology Lectures Week 1

Jan 22nd, 2014 | Comments

My notes on the online Coursera class: Astrobiology and the Search for Extraterrestrial Life.

Week 1 Module 1

1.1 - Introduction

The biggest question of astrobiology is “Are we alone in the Universe?”.

1.2 - The history of astrobiology

We need to be careful about speculation of extraterrestrial life as many great scientists have fallen into this trap in the past.

1.3 - What is Life?

Life is very difficult to define very accurately:
All of the defining characteristics of life are shared by some of the non-living things.
ex. (grows(tornado), replicates(computer virus), metabolizes(forest fire), evolves(computer programs))
There are two types of definitions in philosophy.
- Natural kind - e.g. gold has a precise definition
- Non natural kind - e.g. chair does not have have a precise definition

1.4 - Life on Earth

Physical parameters (temperature, radiation, acidity) defines a biospace. Boundaries of the biospace, the most extreme environments, are occupied by microbes called “extremophiles”.
Life is a thin layer on and just beneath the surface of the Earth.
The upper temperature limit for life is about 120C, making the maximum depth about 5 to 10 kilometers.
There is microbial life on clouds.
Currently active life has only be shown at about 3 km height.
So the earth’s biosphere is only about 10-20 km thick.
Some physical conditions that are suitable for life on earth may overlap with conditions on other planetary bodies.

1.5 - Structure of Life 1

Life on earth is mostly made up from 6 basic elements: carbon, hydrogen, nitrogen, oxygen, phosphorus and sulfur.
Life on earth is carbon and water based.
Water is important because molecules have to move and that is only possible in a liquid medium.

1.6 - The structure of Life 2: Cells

Cells have: membrane, energy system, information system.
If cells didn’t have a membrane, they would simply dissipate into the environment.

Week 1 Module 2

2.1 Origins of Life: Building Blocks

The building blocks of life:
- can be produced in simulated early Earth conditions (Miller–Urey experiment)
- can be found in extraterrestrial materials
Which building blocks came first? Proteins or nucleic acids?

2.2 Origins of Life: Location

Possible locations: deep sea vents, impact craters, beaches, volcanic hot springs.
All these locations might have worked together to produce different building blocks.

2.3 - Origins of Life - Alternate Chemistries

Life on earth is based on carbon.
Advantages of carbon:
- great versatility in the sort of chemical compounds it can form.
- forms stable compounds.
Alternative to carbon: silicon. But not as as favorable as carbon because it forms very stable compounds with oxygen.
Life also needs a solvent.
Advantages of water:
- Wide temperature range
- Floats when freezes
Alternative to carbon: Ammonia. But not as as favorable as water.
The carbon-water biospace seems to be the most plausible or likely to be the most common architecture for other life.
