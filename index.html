Astrobiology Lectures Week 1

Jan 22nd, 2014 | Comments

My notes on the online Coursera class: Astrobiology and the Search for Extraterrestrial Life.

Week 1 Module 1

1.1 - Introduction

The biggest question of astrobiology is “Are we alone in the Universe?”.

1.2 - The history of astrobiology

We need to be careful about speculation of extraterrestrial life as many great scientists have fallen into this trap in the past.

1.3 - What is Life?

Life is very difficult to define very accurately:
All of the defining characteristics of life are shared by some of the non-living things.
ex. (grows(tornado), replicates(computer virus), metabolizes(forest fire), evolves(computer programs))
There are two types of definitions in philosophy.
- Natural kind - e.g. gold has a precise definition
- Non natural kind - e.g. chair does not have have a precise definition

1.4 - Life on Earth

Physical parameters (temperature, radiation, acidity) defines a biospace. Boundaries of the biospace, the most extreme environments, are occupied by microbes called “extremophiles”.
Life is a thin layer on and just beneath the surface of the Earth.
The upper temperature limit for life is about 120C, making the maximum depth about 5 to 10 kilometers.
There is microbial life on clouds.
Currently active life has only be shown at about 3 km height.
So the earth’s biosphere is only about 10-20 km thick.
Some physical conditions that are suitable for life on earth may overlap with conditions on other planetary bodies.

1.5 - Structure of Life 1

Life on earth is mostly made up from 6 basic elements: carbon, hydrogen, nitrogen, oxygen, phosphorus and sulfur.
Life on earth is carbon and water based.
Water is important because molecules have to move and that is only possible in a liquid medium.

1.6 - The structure of Life 2: Cells

Cells have: membrane, energy system, information system.
If cells didn’t have a membrane, they would simply dissipate into the environment.

Week 1 Module 2

2.1 Origins of Life: Building Blocks

The building blocks of life:
- can be produced in simulated early Earth conditions (Miller–Urey experiment)
- can be found in extraterrestrial materials
Which building blocks came first? Proteins or nucleic acids?

2.2 Origins of Life: Location

Possible locations: deep sea vents, impact craters, beaches, volcanic hot springs.
All these locations might have worked together to produce different building blocks.

2.3 - Origins of Life - Alternate Chemistries

Life on earth is based on carbon.
Advantages of carbon:
- great versatility in the sort of chemical compounds it can form.
- forms stable compounds.
Alternative to carbon: silicon. But not as as favorable as carbon because it forms very stable compounds with oxygen.
Life also needs a solvent.
Advantages of water:
- Wide temperature range
- Floats when freezes
Alternative to carbon: Ammonia. But not as as favorable as water.
The carbon-water biospace seems to be the most plausible or likely to be the most common architecture for other life.

Below is a summary of the talk by Robert Greene - Achieving Mastery.

Human brain is a master piece of design which evolved over millions of years. It is an instrument which if you focus deeply on a subject, you understand better and better and more layers of it are revealed to you.

You can’t become a master of something by surfing here and there on the internet.

Thus making connection between ideas is the future of the mastery.

But to become a master of anything, you have to master the basics: Building discipline, being able to practice for a long period of time and being able to focus. Nothing we invent -no drug or application- will ever change this.

How Much Would You Pay to Play This Game?

Dec 28th, 2013 | Comments

Problem

Consider the following game. You are playing a die game where you gain x dollars if the result of the rolled die turns out to be x. If you do not like the outcome, you are given a second try and if you do not like the outcome of the second try, you are given a third try. How much would you pay to play this game?

Solution

As a rational person, you are willing to pay anything less than your expected gain to play this game. And your expected gain depends on your strategy. So first, let’s find the best possible strategy for this game.

If you are in your last try, then you have no choice but to accept the outcome. In this case, your expected gain is $3.5 (\( 3.5 = 1\times \frac{1}{6} + \cdots 6 \times \frac{1}{6}\)). So in your second try, you should only accept a 4, 5 or 6. Because they all beat your expected earning in your last try. So with this best strategy at second try, our expectation is $3.5 if the outcome is 1, 2, 3 and $x if x is 4, 5, 6. This yields an expected gain of \[ 4.25 = 3.5 \times \frac{1}{2} + 4 \times \frac{1}{6} + 5 \times \frac{1}{6} + 6 \times \frac{1}{6} = 1.75 + 2.5 \] in dollars. Hence in your first try, you should only accept a 5 or 6; accepting anything less than your expected gain ($4.25) is meaningless.

So let’s summarize the best strategy: in your first try, only accept 5 or 6. In your second try, only accept 4, 5 or 6. You have no choice for the third try. With this strategy, your expectation is $4.25 if the first die is 1, 2, 3 or 4 and $x if x is 5 or 6. Thus the expectation of the best strategy in dollars is \[ 4.25 \times \frac{4}{6} + 5 \times \frac{1}{6} + 6 \times \frac{1}{6} = 4.67. \]

Thus you should agree to pay anything less than $4.67 to play this game.

This is my shot at the failed Twitter interview problem with a few extras using Python. The problem is this, given an array of wall heights, find the amount of water filled between these walls after a heavy rain. Suppose the given array is [2,5,1,2,3,4,7,7,6], then the corresponding picture is:

       WW 
       WWW
 W     WWW
 W     WWW
 W W   WWW
WW W W WWW
WWWWWWWWWW
2513121776

To get the picture, we define the following function

from __future__ import print_function
def draw_walls(walls):
    """Print an array of wall heights"""
    M = max(walls)  # no of rows
    N = len(walls)  # no of columns
    for i in range(M, 0, -1):
        for j in range(N):
            if walls[j] >= i:
                print('W', end='')
            else:
                print(' ', end='')
        print()
    for i in range(N):
        print(walls[i], end='')

draw_walls([2,5,1,2,3,4,7,7,6])

Hat-check Problem

Dec 24th, 2013 | Comments

Each of \(n\) customers gives a hat to a hat-check person at a restaurant. The hat-check person gives the hats back to the customers in a random order. What is the expected number of customers who get back their own hat?

Solution

Let \(X_i\) be 1 if i-th customer gets own hat back and 0 otherwise. Clearly \[ E[X_i] = 0 \times Pr(X_i = 0) + 1 \times Pr(X_i = 1) = 0 + 1 \times 1/n = 1/n. \] Let \(X\) be the number of customers who get back their own hat. Then By linearity \[ E[X] = \sum_{i=1}^n E[X_i] = 1. \]

Homebrew: A Short Tutorial

Nov 25th, 2013 | Comments

What is it?

After a certain point in my coding life, I had to download new software which often did not come as executables. Usually I found some instructions on websites on how to install these software. But the process was lengthy and demotivating, mostly installing other dependencies and dependencies of those dependencies and running cryptic commands.

Luckily, if you are using a Mac, there are several package managers which do this job automatically for you. The most popular ones are Homebrew and Macports. In this article, I will talk shortly about Homebrew.

Installation

To install Homebrew, type

ruby -e "$(curl -fsSkL raw.github.com/mxcl/homebrew/go)"

into your terminal.

Basic Usage

When you need to install a package, first brew update to update Homebrew formulas.

Then brew install [formula] to install your package.

If an error occurs, try brew doctor which is Homebrew’s own self-diagnosis tool. This may give you hints on to how to fix the problem.

Some Useful Commands

brew upgrade to update installed software

brew uninstall [formula] to uninstall a package

brew list to list installed packages

brew reinstall [formula] will uninstall and install a package

brew tap [repo] opens a new tap(repo) to expand your options of installable software.

Some Useful Packages

There are many packages out there. Here are two I found quite useful when downloading and converting videos from the web.

ffmpeg: video and audio converter

youtube-dl: download videos from youtube.com or other video platforms
